Project Description


Dataset

Our data provider is the United Nations Statistics Division, specifically the United Nations Greenhouse gas Emission Data and United Nations Energy Balance Data dataflows. The data is indexed in 5 broad data flows each sharing the same structure that are indexed by common dimensions (codelists) such as units of the data, the country/area of origin and other labels necessary to identify the quantity that is being reported. For eg. the UN Energy balance database reports total energy (primarily in unit Terajoules) usage of each country among different sources (Commodities) eg. Nuclear Energy, Hydropower etc. and different avenues eg. Imports, Exports etc. In a particular set of identifiers eg. Imports of Nuclear Energy by Afghanistan are tabulated year wise. Each of those dataflows have an annual update schedule and thus it suffices to check for the recency of the database on the application start-up. Since the inconsistency of the number of dimensions and sub-dimensions for any given data point across the different dataflows make it hard to enforce any efficient schema on the dataset. We chose to go the NoSQL way using MongoDB as our database server. In our MongoDB collection UNSD, we have two databases, ebal and unfcc, and two indexes, reference area and year. The unfcc database has 814,160 documents that reveal the commodity transaction over the years. Each document has an assigned unique id, a country, a commodity, a type of transaction, unit of quantity, a year and a value of quantity. The unfcc database has 8,025 documents that show greenhouse gas emission over the years. Each document has an assigned unique id, a type of greenhouse gas, a country, a year and a value of quantity. The unfcc database does not contain all the countries in the ebal database, therefore on our dashboards, you will see some countries do not contain emission data.
